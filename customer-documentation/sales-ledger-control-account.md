This options allows a user to double check the debtor balance in NAS agrees with their manual accounts system / or electronic accounts system. The user will enter a from date and will type in the opening balance (the opening balance is the debtors total on the day of the from date). In this example the opening balance is 330.929.73. This is what the debtor ledger totalled on the 4th Dec. The opening balance will for new clients will be the debtors total from their old system at the point Newline convert the debts into NAS. The closing balance should be the debtors total on the to date. If it is the ledger balances. i.e. everything in the computer system reflects the manual or computerised accounts. All debt and payment data has been entered into both places correctly.

If the figures do not agree and the ledger does not balance the user will need to investigate why. Users can double click on an item to view all the component parts that make up the total. They may need to check off each entry to ensure it agrees with the other system they are matching against. It may be a payment is entered in one system but not another, or entered twice in error crediting an account for example.
