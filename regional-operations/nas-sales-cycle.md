Sales – Survey – Install Cycle

1st Draft:

Introduction:

This document is for internal & reseller use only. Its purpose is to explain the life cycle from lead – sale – site survey – proposal – installation and everything in between. Newline standard daily pricing is £600 + VAT for installation, training & cabling. £640 + vat per day for software writing.

Sales call – questions to client

Number of sites, HQ, auctions, satellite offices

Types of auctions, livestock (cattle, sheep, pigs), plant & machinery, fine art, wine, commercial/industrial

Annual throughput of stock

Auction process, input, cataloguing, sales, financials, balancing etc.

Number of users

Location of users at site

Hardware, no. of / spec of: PC’s, laptops, server(s), switches, router, printers, RFID/EID

Cabling infrastructure, distance, cat5/6, Fibre, Wi-Fi

Renewal date of current contract

Visit site (if possible)

Confirm above points

Demo product

Understand users/decision makers/hierarchy/buying (pain) points

Proposal

See attached examples

Summary of current process

Proposed solution

Itemised pricing

Close call/client visit

Review proposal with client

Refine / tweak

Clarify any objections

Close sale

Client signs sales order form – agreeing to Newline terms of business

Project Management

Hand client over to project manager (PM)

Internal walk through of sales order & client requirement/expectations

PM contacts client

Proposes dates for data capture

Requests data from client, types of sales, stock types, sale dates, commission rates, etc.

PM confirms clients points of contacts

Dates confirmed in NL diary

Hardware purchased (once deposit received)

Newline (NL) – visits site, captures data, to date this has been by:

Access SQL from local backup

Print to file from legacy dos systems

Receive CSV from customer

Installs hardware

Potentially install NL software on existing hardware

Configures for clients specified parameters

Tests links to 3rd party devices/interfaces, e.g. weighbridges, LED displays, government linkups.

Trains staff

Attends 1st live sale

Debrief & follow up site visit
