Unpaid bills increase the sales ledger control account to reflect the auction being owed more money for unpaid bills. A contra reduces the sales ledger as the debt is cleared by the contra. Old debts paid reduce the SLCA as the debts as paid down. The unpaid bills JD figure includes the ven contra JC figure. They balance each other down to 807.21 the actual unpaid figure for the sale.

The double entry for these are:

Unpaid Bills: SLCA JC (ven contra) & part of the pur comm 4005 JC figure & part of the 2104 ven chq figure.

Ven Contra : SLCA JD (part of the unpaid bills)

Old Debts Paid : BANK JD.

Pur Payments is income for the auction into the bank. Old Debts Paid is also income increasing the bank balance and reducing the SLCA.

The double entry for these are:

Pur Payments: the various commission & the VAT & ven cheques figure.

Old Debts Paid: SLCA JC.

Commission is income.

The double entry for these are:

Ven Commission : BANK JD

Entry Fee : BANK JD

Pur Commission : BANK JD

Sales VAT is output VAT. output tax: the VAT you charge on your sales

Purchase VAT is input VAT. input tax: the VAT you pay on your purchases

The difference between output tax and input tax is your VAT liability that you owe to the revenue.

The vendors are paid cheques for the goods they have sold. This is expenditure for the auction.

The double entry for this is:

Total JD 27641.16

Total JC 27641.16
